# 🌐 Network Topologies

## 📌 Overview

Network topology refers to the physical or logical layout of devices in a network. It defines how computers, servers, and other devices are connected and how data flows between them.

Choosing the right topology is important for performance, scalability, cost, and reliability.

---

## 🕸️ Mesh Topology

### 📌 Definition

In a mesh topology, every device is connected to every other device in the network.

### 📌 Advantages

- High reliability (multiple paths for data)  
- No single point of failure  
- Strong fault tolerance  

### 📌 Disadvantages

- Expensive due to large number of connections  
- Complex to install and manage  

### 📌 Use Case

Used in critical systems where reliability is very important, such as military or data centres.

---

## ⭐ Star Topology

### 📌 Definition

In a star topology, all devices are connected to a central device such as a switch or hub.

### 📌 Advantages

- Easy to install and manage  
- Easy to troubleshoot  
- Failure of one device does not affect others  

### 📌 Disadvantages

- Central device failure can bring down the entire network  
- Requires more cable compared to some topologies  

### 📌 Use Case

Most commonly used in modern office and home networks.

---

## 🚌 Bus Topology

### 📌 Definition

In a bus topology, all devices are connected to a single central cable called a backbone.

### 📌 Advantages

- Simple and low cost  
- Requires less cable  

### 📌 Disadvantages

- Difficult to troubleshoot  
- If the main cable fails, the whole network goes down  
- Limited scalability  

### 📌 Use Case

Used in small or temporary networks (rarely used today).

---

## 🔄 Ring Topology

### 📌 Definition

In a ring topology, devices are connected in a circular form where each device is connected to two others.

### 📌 Advantages

- Data flows in an organised manner  
- Reduced data collisions  

### 📌 Disadvantages

- Failure of one device can affect the entire network  
- Difficult to troubleshoot  

### 📌 Use Case

Used in specialised networks, but not common in modern systems.

---

## 🔀 Hybrid Topology

### 📌 Definition

Hybrid topology is a combination of two or more different network topologies.

### 📌 Advantages

- Flexible and scalable  
- Can be customised based on needs  
- Combines benefits of multiple topologies  

### 📌 Disadvantages

- Complex design  
- Higher cost  

### 📌 Use Case

Used in large organisations where different departments may use different topologies.

---

## 📊 Topology Comparison

| Topology | Cost | Reliability | Complexity | Scalability |
|----------|------|------------|------------|------------|
| Mesh     | High | Very High  | High       | Medium     |
| Star     | Medium | High     | Low        | High       |
| Bus      | Low  | Low        | Low        | Low        |
| Ring     | Medium | Medium   | Medium     | Low        |
| Hybrid   | High | High       | High       | High       |

---

## 📌 Recommended Topology

Star topology is the most commonly recommended topology because it is easy to manage, scalable, and reliable for most modern networks.

Hybrid topology is also widely used in large organisations where flexibility is required.

---

## 📌 Real-world Relevance

Network topologies are used in:

- Office networks  
- Data centres  
- Home networks  
- Enterprise environments  

👉 Choosing the right topology improves network performance and reliability.
