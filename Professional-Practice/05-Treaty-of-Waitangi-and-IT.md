# 🇳🇿 Treaty of Waitangi and IT

## 📌 Overview

The Treaty of Waitangi is a foundational document in New Zealand that establishes the relationship between Māori and the Crown. It plays an important role in fairness, respect, and inclusion across all sectors, including IT.

---

## 📜 What is the Treaty of Waitangi?

The Treaty of Waitangi was signed in 1840 between the British Crown and Māori chiefs.

Its purpose was to:

- Establish governance in New Zealand  
- Protect Māori rights and land  
- Promote cooperation between Māori and the Crown  

---

## ⚖️ Three Principles of the Treaty

### 🤝 Partnership
- Work together with Māori in good faith  
- Involve Māori in decision-making  
- Build respectful relationships  

### 🛡️ Protection
- Protect Māori culture, values, and resources  
- Safeguard Māori interests  
- Support language and identity  

### ⚖️ Participation
- Ensure Māori have equal opportunities  
- Encourage involvement in society  
- Remove barriers to inclusion  

---

## 💻 Application of Treaty Principles in IT

### Partnership in IT
- Engage Māori stakeholders in IT projects  
- Collaborate with Māori communities when designing systems  

### Protection in IT
- Protect Māori data and cultural information  
- Respect cultural values in technology design  
- Support privacy and security of sensitive information  

### Participation in IT
- Provide equal access to technology  
- Support digital inclusion  
- Encourage diversity in IT workplaces  

---

## 🔥 Important Line

The Treaty of Waitangi guides IT professionals to work in a fair, inclusive, and culturally respectful manner in New Zealand.

---
