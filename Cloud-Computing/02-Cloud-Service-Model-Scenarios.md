# 🧠 Cloud Service Model Scenarios

## 📌 Overview

This section analyses different business scenarios and identifies the most suitable cloud service model (SaaS, PaaS, or IaaS) based on requirements such as control, flexibility, scalability, security, and cost.

---

## 🧑‍💻 Application Development Scenarios (PaaS)

### Scenario 1: Startup developing a mobile application
**Recommended Model: PaaS**

👉 Justification:  
PaaS allows developers to build and deploy applications without managing infrastructure. This helps the startup focus on development rather than servers and storage.

---

### Scenario 2: Startup building a web app requiring high computing power
**Recommended Model: PaaS**

👉 Justification:  
PaaS provides scalable computing resources and tools needed for application development without requiring physical infrastructure.

---

### Scenario 3: Company developing and deploying a mobile app
**Recommended Model: PaaS**

👉 Justification:  
PaaS enables faster development and deployment while handling backend infrastructure.

---

### Scenario 4: Developers collaborating on a project
**Recommended Model: PaaS**

👉 Justification:  
PaaS allows teams to collaborate, share code, and use development tools efficiently without worrying about hardware resources.

---

## 🏢 Business Application Scenarios (SaaS)

### Scenario 5: Small business setting up website and email
**Recommended Model: SaaS**

👉 Justification:  
SaaS provides ready-to-use services such as email and website hosting without requiring installation or maintenance.

---

### Scenario 6: University providing software access to students
**Recommended Model: SaaS**

👉 Justification:  
SaaS allows students to access applications like Microsoft 365 online without installing software locally.

---

### Scenario 7: Music streaming service delivering content
**Recommended Model: SaaS**

👉 Justification:  
Users can access music through a subscription-based platform over the internet.

---

### Scenario 8: Real estate company managing listings online
**Recommended Model: SaaS**

👉 Justification:  
SaaS platforms provide ready-made systems to manage listings and transactions online.

---

## 🏗️ Infrastructure and Data Control Scenarios (IaaS)

### Scenario 9: Non-profit managing its own database
**Recommended Model: IaaS**

👉 Justification:  
IaaS allows full control over data and infrastructure, which is important for organisations that want to manage their own systems.

---

### Scenario 10: Manufacturing company analysing machine data
**Recommended Model: IaaS**

👉 Justification:  
IaaS provides high-performance computing resources and flexibility for processing large datasets.

---

### Scenario 11: Research group running simulations
**Recommended Model: IaaS**

👉 Justification:  
IaaS offers scalable computing power required for large-scale simulations and experiments.

---

### Scenario 12: Insurance company processing large data
**Recommended Model: IaaS**

👉 Justification:  
IaaS supports big data processing and provides full control over applications and operating systems.

---

### Scenario 13: Media company storing digital assets
**Recommended Model: IaaS**

👉 Justification:  
IaaS offers scalable storage solutions for large files such as images and videos.

---

## 🔒 Special Scenario

### Scenario 14: Government agency storing sensitive data
**Recommended Model: On-Premises**

👉 Justification:  
For highly sensitive data, on-premises solutions provide maximum control and security, avoiding third-party risks.

---

## 📌 Key Learning from Scenarios

- PaaS is best for development and collaboration
- SaaS is best for ready-to-use applications
- IaaS is best for control and large-scale processing
- On-premises is best for highly sensitive data

---

## 🔥 Important Line

Selecting the right cloud service model ensures optimal performance, scalability, and cost efficiency based on business needs.
