# 🔧 PC Assembly and Safety

## 📌 Overview

This document explains the health and safety practices, safe working environment, and step-by-step process for disassembling and assembling a computer system.

These practices are important to prevent injury and avoid damage to sensitive hardware components.

---

## ⚠️ Health and Safety

### 📌 Importance

When working with computer hardware, it is important to follow safety procedures to:

- Protect yourself from injury  
- Prevent damage to components  
- Avoid electrical hazards  

---

### 📌 General Safety Practices

- Always turn off and unplug the computer before working  
- Avoid working with wet hands  
- Keep liquids away from components  
- Use proper tools  
- Keep workspace clean and organised  

---

### 📌 Electrical Safety

- Disconnect power before opening the system  
- Use correct voltage and power supply  
- Do not touch exposed electrical components  

---

## ⚡ Electrostatic Discharge (ESD)

### 📌 What is ESD?

Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) is the sudden flow of static electricity that can damage sensitive computer components.

---

### 📌 Risks of ESD

- Can damage CPU, RAM, and motherboard  
- Damage may not be immediately visible  
- Reduces component lifespan  

---

### 📌 ESD Prevention

- Use an anti-static wrist strap  
- Use an anti-static mat  
- Ground yourself before touching components  
- Avoid working on carpeted surfaces  
- Handle components by edges only  

---

## 🧰 Safe Working Environment

Before starting disassembly or assembly, ensure:

- Clean and clutter-free workspace  
- Adequate lighting  
- Enough space for components  
- Tools are available (screwdrivers, etc.)  
- Components are kept safe from dust and moisture  

👉 Organise screws properly to avoid confusion.

---

## 🔧 Computer Disassembly

### 📌 Purpose

Disassembly is required for:

- Upgrading hardware  
- Repairing components  
- Cleaning the system  

---

### 📌 Steps for Disassembly

```text
Step 1: Turn off the computer
Step 2: Unplug all cables and external devices
Step 3: Remove the side panel of the case
Step 4: Disconnect power supply cables
Step 5: Remove storage devices (HDD/SSD)
Step 6: Remove power supply unit (PSU)
Step 7: Remove expansion cards (GPU, etc.)
Step 8: Remove motherboard
Step 9: Remove RAM modules
Step 10: Remove CPU cooling fan
Step 11: Remove CPU carefully
```
Handle all components carefully and avoid using force.
