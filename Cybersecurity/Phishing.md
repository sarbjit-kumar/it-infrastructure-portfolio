# 🎣 Phishing Attack

## 📌 What is Phishing?
Phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers trick users into providing sensitive information (such as usernames, passwords, or bank details) by pretending to be a trusted source.

---

## ⚙️ How it Works
1. Attacker sends fake email or message  
2. Message looks like a trusted source (bank, company)  
3. User clicks on malicious link  
4. Fake website opens  
5. User enters credentials  
6. Attacker steals the information  

---

## 💥 Impact / Damage
- Theft of personal or financial data  
- Unauthorized access to accounts  
- Financial loss  
- Data breaches in organisations  

---

## 🛡️ Prevention / Protection
- Use **2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)**  
- Avoid clicking suspicious links  
- Verify website URLs before login  
- Do not open unknown attachments  
- Use antivirus and email filtering  

---

## 🧠 Key Concepts
- Phishing uses **social engineering**  
- Relies on human trust  
- Common entry point for cyber attacks  

---

## 🔗 Integration with IT Systems
- MFA reduces risk of stolen credentials  
- Monitoring helps detect unusual logins  
- Security policies improve user protection  

---

## 💼 Real-world Relevance
Phishing is one of the most common cyber attacks affecting individuals and organisations worldwide.

---

## 🔥 Important Line
Phishing attacks exploit human behaviour to steal sensitive information through fake communication.

---

## 🧠 Simple Understanding
Phishing = “fake message to steal your data”

---

## 🎤 Interview Line
Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers trick users into providing sensitive information using fake emails or websites.
