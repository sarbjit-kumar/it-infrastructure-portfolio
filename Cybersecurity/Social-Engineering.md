# 🧠 Social Engineering Attack

## 📌 What is Social Engineering?
Social engineering is a cyber attack that manipulates people into revealing sensitive information by exploiting human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities.

---

## ⚙️ How it Works
1. Attacker gathers information about the target  
2. Builds trust with the victim  
3. Tricks the victim into sharing sensitive data  
4. Uses the information for malicious purposes  

---

## 🔹 Common Types
- **Phishing** → Fake emails or messages  
- **Pretexting** → Pretending to be a trusted person  
- **Baiting** → Using infected USB or files  

---

## 💥 Impact / Damage
- Data theft  
- Unauthorized system access  
- Financial loss  
- Business compromise  

---

## 🛡️ Prevention / Protection
- Do not trust unknown emails or calls  
- Verify identity before sharing information  
- Avoid using unknown USB devices  
- Educate users about attacks  
- Use security awareness training  

---

## 🧠 Key Concepts
- Targets human behaviour, not systems  
- Relies on trust and manipulation  
- Common starting point of cyber attacks  

---

## 🔗 Integration with IT Systems
- GPO can enforce restrictions  
- MFA reduces risk  
- Monitoring detects suspicious activity  

---

## 💼 Real-world Relevance
Social engineering is one of the most common causes of security breaches in organisations.

---

## 🔥 Important Line
Social engineering attacks exploit human behaviour to gain unauthorized access.

---

## 🧠 Simple Understanding
Social Engineering = “tricking people instead of hacking systems”

---

## 🎤 Interview Line
Social engineering is a technique where attackers manipulate users into revealing sensitive information instead of exploiting technical vulnerabilities.
